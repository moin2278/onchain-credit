Borrower risk research — Aave V3, Ethereum

Wallet reputation does not predict liquidation. Borrower behavior does.

We backtested wallet-level risk scoring against 18 months of real Aave V3 liquidations — and published the failures along with the result. Hand-tuned "reputation" scores carried zero signal. A fitted model on borrowing history and behavior predicts 90-day liquidation at borrow time.

Exhibit B — The backtest

Three rounds, one honest number

Every round is reproducible from public data with the open-source pipeline. Round 2 was discarded after we found a confound in our own design.

RoundDesignOutcome
1Hand-tuned reputation score (age, activity, diversity) No signal — HIGH tier liquidated 48.6% vs LOW 54.4%
2Fitted model, liquidation-anchored AUC 0.74 — rejected: anchor-selection confound
3Fitted model, borrow-anchored (production framing) CV AUC 0.720 ± 0.057 · held-out 62.9% vs 33.3%
  • Liquidated borrowers are older and more active than safe ones (median wallet age 522 vs 105 days). Sophistication correlates with leverage, not safety.
  • Borrower inexperience is the strongest risk marker. First-time borrowers are liquidated at far higher rates — novice risk shows up onchain like it does in traditional credit.
  • Stablecoin-heavy flows lean risk, consistent with leverage looping — the opposite of the usual scoring assumption.
